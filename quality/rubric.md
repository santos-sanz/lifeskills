# Cross-skill evaluation rubric (1-5)

Use this rubric to score any skill response. A passing response should be decision-ready, evidence-aware, and explicit about risk.

## Scoring scale
- 1: Poor. Major structural and content failures.
- 2: Weak. Several important gaps; not decision-ready.
- 3: Adequate. Usable but missing rigor in key areas.
- 4: Strong. Clear, actionable, and mostly rigorous.
- 5: Excellent. High clarity, rigor, and practical execution value.

## Criteria

## 1) Trigger fit
- 1: Wrong framework for the request.
- 3: Partially relevant framework.
- 5: Exact fit for user intent and decision type.

## 2) Actionability
- 1: Generic advice with no executable steps.
- 3: Some concrete steps, limited ownership/timing.
- 5: Specific actions, owners, timing, and success signals.

## 3) Clarity and structure
- 1: Disorganized and hard to scan.
- 3: Understandable but uneven structure.
- 5: Crisp flow with clear sectioning and prioritization.

## 4) Evidence discipline
- 1: Claims without support.
- 3: Some support but weak source quality signaling.
- 5: Claims linked to evidence with confidence and gaps clearly marked.

## 5) Risk handling
- 1: No risk analysis.
- 3: Risks listed but weak mitigations.
- 5: Material risks with practical mitigations and contingencies.

## 6) Missing-information handling
- 1: Ignores critical unknowns.
- 3: Asks questions but cannot proceed productively.
- 5: Asks up to critical questions, then proceeds with explicit assumptions.

## 7) Output contract adherence
- 1: Missing required sections.
- 3: Sections present but uneven quality.
- 5: Complete and coherent sections in required order.

## Acceptance thresholds
- Average per skill >= 4.2
- No criterion below 3.5 for any evaluated prompt
- Zero critical guardrail failures
