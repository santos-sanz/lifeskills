# Example: HR software expansion with uncertain market data

## Context
- Decision to support: Whether to enter North American mid-market healthcare segment this year.
- Market definition and scope: Cloud HRIS platforms for firms with 200-2,500 employees.
- Geography and segments: US and Canada; healthcare and adjacent regulated services.
- Time horizon: 24 months.

## Decision or recommendation
- Recommended strategic direction: Enter with a focused compliance-first offer targeting healthcare providers with 500-1,500 employees.
- Why this option now: Segment shows sustained demand for audit-ready workflows and has weaker fit with broad horizontal incumbents.
- Confidence level: Medium.

## Analysis
### Market sizing triangulation
- TAM (top-down): Estimated 7.2B software spend in scope industries.
- TAM (bottom-up): Estimated 6.6B based on account count x ACV assumptions.
- SAM: 2.9B after segment and feature-fit filtering.
- SOM: 180-260M reachable in 24 months under partner-led GTM.
- Reconciliation notes: Range retained due to uncertainty in implementation service attach rates.

### Market dynamics
- Growth drivers: Compliance complexity, labor turnover, and shift from legacy suites.
- Structural constraints: Long buying cycles and integration burden.
- External factors (PESTEL highlights): Regulatory pressure increases demand but raises procurement scrutiny.

### Competitive landscape
- Direct competitors: Workday, UKG, BambooHR, niche healthcare HR vendors.
- Substitutes and adjacent threats: Payroll-led bundles and outsourced HR service providers.
- Pricing and positioning matrix: Incumbents cluster high price/high breadth; niche players cluster medium price/high compliance depth.
- Capability signals (distribution, product depth, brand trust): Incumbents lead distribution; niche vendors lead vertical workflow credibility.

### Scenario view
- Base scenario: Partner-led entry reaches 35M ARR by year 2.
- Stressed scenario: Procurement delays cut year-2 ARR by 30 percent.
- Key sensitivity variable: Time-to-value in first implementation cycle.

## Risks
- Risk: Underestimated implementation complexity in regulated workflows.
- Mitigation: Launch with limited scope package and certified partner onboarding.
- Risk: Price pressure from bundled incumbents.
- Mitigation: Position on compliance outcome and deployment speed, not feature parity.

## Next actions
- Analysis task: Validate willingness-to-pay and switching friction with 12 target accounts.
- Owner: Strategy lead.
- Date: March 28.
- Success signal: At least 8 of 12 accounts confirm high-value compliance pain with budget intent.

## Assumptions
- Assumption: Mid-market healthcare buyers will pay premium for faster compliance reporting setup.
- Confidence (high/medium/low): Medium.
- Validation needed: Structured buyer interviews and pilot proposal feedback.
