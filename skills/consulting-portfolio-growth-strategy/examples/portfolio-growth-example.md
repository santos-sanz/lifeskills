# Example: portfolio reset under cash constraints

## Context
- Portfolio units in scope: Core SaaS, Services, Legacy On-Prem.
- Strategic objective: Restore growth while preserving cash resilience.
- Constraints (budget, talent, risk): New investment budget capped at 12M; hiring frozen for 2 quarters.
- Time horizon: 18 months.

## Decision or recommendation
- Recommended allocation posture: Concentrate investment in Core SaaS, protect Services as retention engine, accelerate harvest of Legacy On-Prem.
- Capital split summary: Invest 65 percent Core SaaS, Hold 20 percent Services, Harvest 15 percent Legacy.
- Why this allocation now: Core shows strongest scalable economics while Legacy consumes disproportionate maintenance cost.

## Analysis
### Framework and scoring
- Framework selected: GE/McKinsey for primary allocation, Ansoff for growth move design.
- Attractiveness criteria and weights: Segment growth 35 percent, margin potential 25 percent, strategic adjacency 20 percent, regulatory burden 20 percent.
- Competitive position criteria and weights: Relative share 30 percent, product differentiation 30 percent, channel strength 20 percent, delivery capability 20 percent.

### Portfolio map
- Core SaaS classification and score: Invest; high attractiveness, medium-high strength.
- Services classification and score: Hold; medium attractiveness, high strength.
- Legacy On-Prem classification and score: Harvest; low attractiveness, medium strength.

### Allocation logic
- Invest: Core SaaS roadmap acceleration and partner distribution.
- Hold: Services bundle redesign to support SaaS retention.
- Harvest: Legacy price uplift and support scope rationalization.
- Exit: No immediate exit; reevaluate after 2 checkpoints.

### Growth move sequencing
- Move 1: Launch vertical bundle for top-performing SaaS segment (owner: GM Core, dependency: pricing ops, timing: Q2).
- Move 2: Attach success services to new SaaS deals (owner: Services Director, dependency: enablement package, timing: Q2-Q3).
- Move 3: Decommission low-margin legacy custom modules (owner: CTO, dependency: customer migration plans, timing: Q3).

## Risks
- Risk: aggressive legacy harvest triggers churn spike.
- Mitigation: stage price changes and offer migration credits to SaaS.
- Risk: partner channel ramp slower than planned.
- Mitigation: maintain direct sales fallback for top 20 accounts.

## Next actions
- Action: Confirm weighted scoring with finance and BU leaders.
- Owner: Strategy PMO.
- Date: March 18.
- Success signal: Executive sign-off on allocation and milestones in next operating review.

## Assumptions
- Assumption: Core SaaS win rates remain stable during resource reallocation.
- Confidence (high/medium/low): Medium.
- Validation needed: Monthly win-rate monitoring by segment.
