# High agency principles

## 1) Reality has no quicksand
- Treat most "impossible" claims as untested assumptions.
- Assume movement is possible until constraints are proven hard.
- Default question: "What would make this solvable in the next 72 hours?"

## 2) Reject learned helplessness
- Replace passive framing ("we can't") with agency framing ("what would need to be true?").
- Ban low-resolution excuses; require evidence for each blocker.
- Reward actions that create options, not narratives that defend inaction.

## 3) Make constraints specific
- Convert generic blockers into numbers, owners, and dates.
- Distinguish hard constraints (laws, cash runway, physics) from soft constraints (norms, habits, assumptions).
- Every soft constraint gets a test plan.

## 4) Convert ambiguity into variables and bets
- Define unknowns as variables with ranges.
- Turn each variable into a cheap testable bet.
- Prefer reversible experiments before irreversible commitments.

## 5) Bias to action under uncertainty
- Ship small probes quickly, then iterate from feedback.
- Reduce cycle time between decision, action, and learning.
- Keep momentum by assigning explicit owners and review checkpoints.

## 6) Agency compounds through exposure
- Increase luck surface area by publishing work, making asks, and creating more interactions with reality.
- Favor output cadence over perfection loops.
- Measure agency by speed of learning and quality of next move.
