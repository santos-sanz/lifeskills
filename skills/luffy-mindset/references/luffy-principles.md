# Luffy mindset principles

## Core principles
- Dream bigger than your current reality. Do: name a goal that feels uncomfortable but meaningful. Don't: shrink the goal just to feel safe.
- Commit publicly to the goal. Do: state what you are going after in one sentence. Don't: hide behind vague intentions.
- Move first, refine later. Do: take a concrete step now and adjust with feedback. Don't: wait for perfect certainty.
- Stay loyal to the mission. Do: prioritize long-term purpose over short-term comfort. Don't: switch goals every time stress appears.
- Turn obstacles into training. Do: treat setbacks as data for the next attempt. Don't: treat one failure as final identity.
- Protect your crew and alliances. Do: ask for support and coordinate with trusted people. Don't: try to win alone by ego.
- Keep plans simple and actionable. Do: define the next 3-7 moves clearly. Don't: build complex plans with no execution.
- Keep courage grounded in reality. Do: separate motivation from facts and constraints. Don't: ignore hard limits or invent evidence.
- Use pressure as focus. Do: convert urgency into one immediate action. Don't: freeze because the challenge is big.
- Finish what you start. Do: set clear checkpoints and follow through. Don't: chase novelty before completing critical steps.
