# Example: enterprise vendor renewal under budget pressure

## Context
- Negotiation type and stakes: Annual software renewal with 1.8M spend exposure and potential scope reduction.
- Parties and decision process: Procurement lead, IT sponsor, and CFO sign-off on buyer side; account executive and regional director on seller side.
- Timeline and pressure points: Renewal closes in 19 days; buyer has budget freeze mandate.

## Decision or recommendation
- Recommended strategy posture: Protect core value and trade selectively on term and expansion timing instead of broad price cuts.
- Target outcome: Keep at least 95 percent ARR with multi-year commitment and phased module rollout.
- Walk-away boundary: No agreement below 88 percent ARR equivalent without compensating term or scope protections.

## Analysis
### Strategy layer
- BATNA: Extend existing footprint with reduced module set for one quarter while running parallel replacement assessment.
- ZOPA estimate (conservative/optimistic): Conservative 88-92 percent ARR, optimistic 93-98 percent ARR.
- Counterparty incentives and constraints: Procurement needs immediate savings signal; IT needs continuity and low migration risk.
- Key leverage points: Switching cost, implementation stability, and audit reliability requirements.

### Script layer
- Opening label: "It sounds like you are under pressure to show immediate savings this cycle."
- Calibrated how/what questions: "What would a defensible savings plan look like for your CFO without increasing operational risk?"
- Mirror prompts: "Defensible savings plan?"
- Accusation audit statements: "You may think we are protecting margin at your expense and not taking your budget constraints seriously."
- Summary to reach "that's right": "You need near-term budget relief, but you cannot risk disruption before audit season. Did I get that right?"

### Concession ladder
- Concession 1 (condition required): Offer 3 percent price relief if term extends to 24 months.
- Concession 2 (condition required): Delay billing for add-on module if renewal scope remains intact.
- Non-negotiables: Service-level terms and compliance support package.

## Risks
- Risk: Procurement uses deadline pressure to force unilateral concession.
- Mitigation: Re-anchor on risk transfer and conditional trade framework.
- Risk: Hidden decision maker introduces new conditions late.
- Mitigation: Confirm decision chain and approval criteria in writing after each call.

## Next actions
- Action: Send recap with conditional concession matrix and requested decision checkpoints.
- Owner: Account executive.
- Date: Within 2 hours post-call.
- Success signal: Buyer confirms acceptable option set and final approver meeting date.

## Assumptions
- Assumption: Buyer values implementation continuity more than incremental price delta beyond 5 percent.
- Confidence (high/medium/low): Medium.
- Validation needed: Probe migration risk appetite in next stakeholder call.
