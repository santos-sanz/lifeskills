# Example: salary negotiation

## Context
- Situation: Annual review for senior engineer
- Parties: Employee and manager
- Stakes: Comp and scope

## Objective and limits
- Primary objective: 12 percent base increase
- Minimum acceptable: 8 percent
- Walk-away: stay in role, re-open in 3 months

## Counterparty map
- Decision makers: Manager, HR
- Incentives: Budget control, retention
- Likely emotions: Cautious about precedent

## Tactical script
- Opening: "It sounds like budgets are tight this cycle."
- Calibrated question: "How can we make a retention case that fits this budget?"
- Accusation audit: "You may think I am being aggressive or unrealistic."
- Summary: "It seems you need to stay within banding, but you want to keep me. Is that right?"

## Concessions and trades
- Concessions: split increase into base + bonus, adjust title scope
- Trade rules: only trade for timeline commitment
