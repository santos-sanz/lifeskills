# Example: paid-plan upgrade campaign with evidence constraints

## Context
- Objective: Increase trial starts from free teams in the next 30 days.
- Audience: Operations leads at small and mid-sized distributed teams.
- Desired action and deadline: Start 14-day trial by month end.
- Channel and constraints: Email and in-app banner; legal prohibits unverified performance claims.

## Decision or recommendation
- Recommended persuasion strategy: Combine social proof, authority, and commitment with transparent trial framing.
- Selected principles: Social proof, authority, commitment and consistency.
- Why this mix fits this audience: Audience is risk-aware and responds to credible peer adoption plus low-friction first step.

## Analysis
### Barrier and motivator map
- Key barrier 1: Concern that migration effort will outweigh productivity gains.
- Key motivator 1: Need to standardize workflows across distributed teams.
- Key barrier 2: Skepticism toward promotional claims.
- Key motivator 2: Preference for evidence from similar companies.

### Principle -> evidence -> claim matrix
- Principle: Social proof.
- Evidence source and quality: Verified CRM data showing 1,124 similar teams upgraded in the last two quarters (high).
- Claim wording: "Over 1,100 operations teams like yours moved to paid workflows this year."

- Principle: Authority.
- Evidence source and quality: Independent analyst report ranking product in top tier for reliability (medium-high).
- Claim wording: "Independent analysts ranked our workflow reliability among the top options for growing teams."

- Principle: Commitment and consistency.
- Evidence source and quality: Internal onboarding data showing teams that complete setup checklist adopt 2.3x more features (medium).
- Claim wording: "Start with a 15-minute setup checklist to lock in immediate value during your trial."

### Message variants
- Variant A (channel-specific draft + CTA): Email subject "Join 1,100+ teams improving ops consistency"; body emphasizes peer adoption, analyst trust marker, and simple first-step CTA: "Start your 14-day trial."
- Variant B (channel-specific draft + CTA): In-app banner with concise proof and low-friction action: "Teams like yours standardize faster here. Start trial."

## Risks
- Risk: social proof interpreted as non-comparable if audience segment mismatch is unclear.
- Mitigation: specify segment similarity in message footnote.
- Risk: authority claim perceived as vague.
- Mitigation: include report name and date where channel allows.

## Next actions
- Action: Launch A/B test for two variants and track trial start plus activation.
- Owner: Lifecycle marketing manager.
- Date: March 6.
- Success signal: Trial start rate up at least 20 percent with no increase in early churn.

## Assumptions
- Assumption: Segment similarity criteria in CRM match campaign targeting logic.
- Confidence (high/medium/low): Medium.
- Validation needed: Pre-launch audience QA with RevOps.
