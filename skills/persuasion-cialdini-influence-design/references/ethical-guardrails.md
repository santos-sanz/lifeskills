# Ethical guardrails for persuasion design

## Non-negotiable rules
- No false claims, fabricated proof, or hidden conflicts of interest.
- No deceptive urgency or fake scarcity.
- No coercive framing, threats, or consent bypass.
- No misrepresentation of authority, credentials, or endorsements.

## Risk checks before publishing
- Claim-evidence alignment: each claim must be supportable.
- Audience vulnerability check: avoid exploiting fear or asymmetry.
- Reversibility check: action should be reasonably reversible when possible.
- Transparency check: disclose incentives or important limitations.

## Manipulation warning signs
- Pressure language disconnected from user benefit.
- Social proof without segment relevance.
- Authority cited without source and date.
- CTA that obscures cost, commitment, or data use.

## Safe alternatives
- Replace pressure with clear value and informed choice.
- Offer neutral comparison framing when uncertainty is high.
- Use explicit opt-out and plain-language terms.

## Escalation rule
If ethical risk remains high after edits, recommend not launching the message until proof and framing are corrected.
