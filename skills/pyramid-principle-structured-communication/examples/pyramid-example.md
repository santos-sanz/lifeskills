# Example: renewal pricing decision under incomplete data

## Context
- Situation summary: The enterprise segment remains the largest profit pool, but gross margin fell 4 points in two quarters.
- Complication that creates urgency: Hosting and support costs rose faster than contract value, and FY planning closes in 3 weeks.
- Governing question (single): Should we adjust enterprise pricing this quarter?
- Decision owner: CFO

## Decision or recommendation
- BLUF: Increase enterprise list price by 4 percent now, with controlled discount guardrails, while preserving existing multi-year customers.
- Decision requested: Approve pricing update for new enterprise quotes effective next month.
- Why now: Waiting one more quarter likely locks in another low-margin renewal cycle.

## Analysis
### Support point 1 (parallel phrasing)
- Claim: Demand signal remains resilient in target accounts.
- Evidence (fact/source/quality): Pipeline and win rate stayed stable in the top two enterprise verticals (medium confidence; last 90 days only).
- Implication: A measured increase is unlikely to create immediate volume shock.

### Support point 2 (parallel phrasing)
- Claim: Relative price position is still below direct peers.
- Evidence (fact/source/quality): Sales intelligence indicates two primary competitors raised effective enterprise pricing in the last two quarters (medium confidence; external estimates).
- Implication: The market anchor likely supports a moderate move.

### Support point 3 (parallel phrasing)
- Claim: Margin pressure will persist without intervention.
- Evidence (fact/source/quality): Internal finance trend shows sustained cost inflation in cloud and premium support lines (high confidence).
- Implication: Delaying action erodes FY target attainment.

## Risks
- Risk 1: Sales teams over-discount and neutralize the increase.
- Mitigation 1: Tighten approval thresholds and track deal-level discount exceptions weekly.
- Risk 2: Strategic accounts push back on renewal timing.
- Mitigation 2: Preserve current price for committed multi-year renewals and trade for term length.

## Next actions
- Action: Launch price update playbook and approval matrix.
- Owner: VP Revenue Operations.
- Date: March 15.
- Success signal: Net realized enterprise price up at least 2 percent by end of quarter.

## Assumptions
- Assumption: Enterprise churn sensitivity remains low at a 4 percent list increase.
- Confidence (high/medium/low): Medium.
- Validation needed: Run a 30-day cohort comparison by segment and region.
